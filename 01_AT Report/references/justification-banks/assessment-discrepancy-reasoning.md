# Pattern — reconciling conflicting assessment scores

When two clinicians record different scores for the same measure (commonly MMT), don't quietly pick one and drop the other, and don't average them. Report both, explain the likely cause, then pivot to functional evidence.

## Why this happens

MMT results vary with the examiner, testing posture, compensation allowed, fatigue level at the time of testing, and — particularly in fluctuating conditions like MS — genuine variability in presentation day to day. This is clinically normal, not a data error, but an unexplained discrepancy reads to an assessor as "the evidence is inconsistent."

## How to write it up

> "There is variability in the documented [measure] across assessments. [Assessor A] recorded [score]; [Assessor B] recorded [score]. This difference likely reflects variation in testing conditions, fatigue levels, examiner technique, and the fluctuating nature of [condition]. Regardless of the numerical grading recorded by different clinicians, all assessments consistently identified clinically significant [functional deficit] affecting gait safety, efficiency, and community mobility."

Then anchor the clinical recommendation to the **functional** evidence (observed foot drop, reduced toe clearance, tripping episodes, compensatory gait) rather than to either isolated number. Explicitly state this pivot — it pre-empts two questions an assessor might otherwise raise: "if the score is 0/5, why did movement occur?" and "if the score is 3/5, why is AT still needed?" Framing the case around functional foot drop rather than a specific muscle grade answers both at once.

## Where this applies

Only the *specific* sections affected need updating when new information arrives (e.g. assessment findings, trial outcomes, clinical justification) — a discrepancy discovered late doesn't require rewriting the whole report, just the sections that cite the number directly.
