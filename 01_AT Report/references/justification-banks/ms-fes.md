# Justification bank — MS + FES (Bioness)

Reusable Section 34 (Reasonable & Necessary) language. Customise the bracketed detail per participant — don't submit these verbatim.

## 1. Related to disability
The requested AT directly relates to [participant]'s MS. Foot drop, ankle instability, fatigue and gait instability are neurological consequences of MS. [Device] directly addresses these impairments by stimulating the common peroneal nerve to assist dorsiflexion and foot clearance during gait.

## 2. Effective and beneficial
The device has demonstrated clear functional benefit during in-clinic and community trials: [cite this participant's actual TUG/6MWT/10MWT/DGI changes]. Functional deterioration during washout further confirms benefit was attributable to the device, not incidental.

## 3. Value for money
Although the device is high-cost, the trial demonstrates it is likely to reduce longer-term support needs by improving mobility, reducing fall risk, reducing carer reliance, and supporting ongoing participation. Funding the device is more cost-effective than unmanaged deterioration, increased falls risk, increased support-worker hours, or avoidable injury. **Back this with an actual figure where possible** — a purely clinical argument without financial comparison is a known weak point (see `../submission-compliance-checklist.md`).

## 4. Most appropriate support
Alternative options have been considered and are insufficient: [name the specific AFO/mobility aid trialled and why it failed for this participant]. Physiotherapy alone cannot compensate for absent/reduced voluntary dorsiflexion strength. No intervention would leave [participant] at high risk of falls, reduced mobility, and increased dependence.

## 5. Supports NDIS goals
[Device] supports [participant]'s stated goals to increase mobility, improve stability, reduce falls risk, increase independence, improve home safety, and participate more confidently in community life. Link to the participant's actual goal statement (Appendix G), don't paraphrase generically.

## Multi-component devices (Lower Leg + Thigh, or similar)

If recommending more than one component, justify each separately — assessors commonly question the second/optional component first. State what the base component alone did *not* resolve (e.g. knee hyperextension in stance persisting despite improved swing-phase clearance), and what was observed when the additional component was added during trial vs what returned during washout without it.

## Reframing low fall incidence

"[Participant]'s low incidence of falls prior to the trial should not be interpreted as evidence of safety. Rather, it reflects the extent to which [participant] has restricted activity and modified behaviour to avoid situations that increase risk of tripping, fatigue, and loss of balance."
