# Clinical note → NDIS-ready phrasing

The single biggest time-saver in the whole workflow: capture findings in NDIS language *at the point of assessment*, not when writing the report later.

| Instead of (clinical shorthand) | Write (NDIS-ready) |
|---|---|
| "Foot drop present." | "Foot drop resulting in reduced toe clearance, increasing falls risk and limiting safe community ambulation." |
| "Reduced DF strength." | "Reduced active ankle dorsiflexion contributing to impaired swing-phase clearance and gait instability." |
| A bare MMT score | The score plus its functional consequence — assessors weight functional evidence over isolated muscle grades (see `justification-banks/assessment-discrepancy-reasoning.md`) |

## Reusable stock lines

- "Without this device, the participant is at increased risk of falls and injury."
- "This limitation significantly impacts independent mobility and community access."
- "A lower-cost alternative is not suitable because [specific reason tied to this participant's presentation, not a generic statement]."
- "[Participant]'s low prior incidence of falls should not be read as evidence of safety — it reflects the extent to which they have restricted activity and adopted compensatory strategies to avoid situations that increase risk."

## Workflow comparison (why this matters)

| Slower way | Faster way |
|---|---|
| Write clinical notes only ("foot drop present") | Capture NDIS-ready notes at assessment time |
| Draft justification from scratch each time | Pull from `justification-banks/<condition>.md`, customise ~20–30% |
| Format outcome tables at the end | Populate outcome tables during the assessment itself |
| Search for research papers per case | Cite from `evidence-summary-<condition>.md` |

This reduces billable administrative time by roughly 30–50% while improving Section 34 justification quality — it isn't just a writing-speed trick, weak NDIS phrasing is a real rejection risk.

## Report-writing time (for scheduling / billing estimates)

- Simple/low-cost AT (e.g. basic AFO): 0.5–1 hr
- Standard AT report: 1.5–3 hrs (most common)
- Complex/high-cost, progressive-condition cases (MS, ALS, CP, multi-component devices): 3–6+ hrs

Billable under NDIS Capacity Building – Improved Daily Living when clinically necessary and documented: assessment, AT trial, report writing, liaison with other clinicians, outcome review. Often non-billable or disputed: excessive formatting/admin, rewrites caused by weak initial documentation, full rewrites after rejection — which is exactly what the phrasing above and `submission-compliance-checklist.md` are meant to prevent.
