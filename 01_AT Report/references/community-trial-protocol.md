# 8-week community trial protocol (Bioness / FES)

Structures the trial to prove four things at once: safety, effectiveness, functional change, and NDIS evidence (trial vs withdrawal comparison).

## Week 0 — baseline

Complete the full checklist in `outcome-measures.md` before device fitting. Also record: current AFO/mobility-aid use, current indoor/outdoor walking distance, and existing compensatory gait strategies.

## Phase 1 — Adaptation (Weeks 1–2)

**Goal:** stimulation tolerance and skin integrity.
- 30–60 min/day indoors, flat ground.
- Clinician optimises stimulation level and heel-switch sensitivity for functional dorsiflexion with eversion.
- Monitor: skin response, excessive fatigue, balance changes.

## Phase 2 — Functional expansion (Weeks 3–4)

**Goal:** community ambulation.
- 1–2 hrs/day, including outdoor terrain (ramps, driveways, footpaths).
- Compare against the participant's existing AFO in the same settings — note where the device is clearly better.
- Mid-trial re-test: 10MWT, TUG.

## Phase 3 — Validation (Weeks 5–6)

**Goal:** "I need this" threshold — high-demand activities.
- 2–4 hrs/day continuous wear where tolerated, in the hardest real situations (shopping, public transport, work/school commute).
- Have the participant articulate, in their own words, the specific advantage over the trial AFO (bulk, fatigue, confidence).

## Phase 4 — Evidence collection (Weeks 7–8)

**Goal:** finalise data for the NDIS submission.
- Final 10MWT / TUG / 6MWT, PSFS goal review.
- Document participation gains explicitly (e.g. "able to stand long enough to bake," "increased weekly outings") — these carry more weight with assessors than raw gait-speed numbers alone.

## Daily diary (ask the participant to keep this throughout)

Usage time, location (home / outdoor / community), how it felt (stability ↑ / fatigue ↓ / pain ↓), and any problems. Concrete, specific entries are far more persuasive than general statements — e.g. "walked through the supermarket pushing the trolley without my foot catching; with the AFO I was fatigued within 10 minutes" beats "felt better today."

## Post-trial washout (3 weeks, after device return)

**Purpose:** prove the trial gains were caused by the device, not incidental — deterioration on withdrawal is some of the strongest NDIS evidence available.

Daily record: where they walked, activities done, walking difficulty (very difficult/difficult/manageable/easy), felt unstable (Y/N), safety incidents (toe drag/tripping/near-fall/fall), comparison to on-device walking (much worse/worse/same), fatigue (much more/more/same), activities avoided that day.

Weekly summary: walking safety status, walking endurance status, fatigue/effort status, participation impact (list activities now avoided).

Final reflection (end of 3 weeks): how has walking changed since device return; what's now harder or impossible without it; do they feel less safe; do they feel they *need* the device for safe daily mobility; how would non-provision affect their independence.
