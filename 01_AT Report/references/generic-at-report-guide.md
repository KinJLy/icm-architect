# Generic AT report guide (non-condition-specific)

Use when starting a case that isn't MS+FES or CP+AFO — the general shape both of those specialise from.

## Information to gather

1. **Participant & context** — NDIS number, diagnosis, functional impacts (mobility, fatigue, balance, falls risk), living situation, current mobility aids.
2. **Baseline (before trial/intervention)** — walking distance, gait pattern, speed/endurance if measurable, falls history, fatigue, participation (shopping, outdoor access, work/appointments, stairs/uneven ground).
3. **Trial details** (if applicable) — dates, model/device, wearing schedule, training provided, support required, issues encountered.
4. **Outcomes** — objective changes (speed, distance, tripping, clearance, symmetry, reliance on aids) and subjective changes (confidence, fear of falling, fatigue, pain, willingness to go out, quality of life) from participant, carer, and clinician.
5. **Comparison to alternatives** — no device, current device, physiotherapy alone: why each is insufficient, why the recommended AT is superior for *this* participant specifically.
6. **Risk & safety** — falls risk with/without, safety on stairs/ramps/uneven terrain, risk of deconditioning if not funded, carer safety.
7. **Independence & participation** — link directly to the participant's stated NDIS goals.
8. **Cost & value for money** — device cost, expected lifespan, ongoing costs, comparison against ongoing therapy/injury/support-hour costs.
9. **Clinical recommendation** — explicit "reasonable and necessary," not luxury/convenience, consequences if not funded.
10. **Supporting evidence to attach** — trial summary, gait assessment, referral/specialist letter, falls record, participant statement.

## Orthotics-specific additions (any AFO/KAFO/spinal orthosis/splint case)

Cover: previous orthoses used and their outcomes/limitations; joint ROM, strength, tone/spasticity, sensation, skin condition, postural alignment; device design detail (type, laterality, materials, joints, straps, footwear requirements); training/implementation plan (fitting schedule, wearing-in schedule, education provided, follow-up schedule); expected outcomes framed as safety/endurance/reduced-carer-reliance/confidence/participation, not just "improved gait."

For **why custom, not off-the-shelf**: state the specific anatomical reason (deformity, bony prominence, contracture) — generic statements about comfort don't hold up as well as a specific fit problem.
