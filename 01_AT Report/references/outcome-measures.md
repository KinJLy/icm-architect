# Outcome measures — definitions and baseline checklist

Collect these at baseline (Week 0, before device fitting), mid-trial, end-of-trial, and again at end of washout. Same measures every time — a comparison table only works if the rows match.

## Strength, tone, range

- **Manual Muscle Testing (MMT)** — dorsiflexion (DF), plantarflexion (PF), eversion (EV), inversion (IV), knee flexion/extension, hip abduction/extension. Record which assessor and in what posture/context — MMT is known to vary between examiners and with fatigue, especially in fluctuating conditions like MS (see `justification-banks/assessment-discrepancy-reasoning.md` if scores conflict).
- **Modified Ashworth Scale (MAS)** — spasticity/tone, by muscle group.
- **Range of Motion (ROM)** — especially ankle DF with knee extended vs flexed.

## Fatigue

- **Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS)** — 9 items, 1–7 each, max 63. A 63/63 baseline indicates maximum measurable fatigue burden — state this explicitly rather than just reporting the number.

## Mobility / functional

- **10 Metre Walk Test (10MWT)** — comfortable and fast pace.
- **Timed Up and Go (TUG)**
- **6 Minute Walk Test (6MWT)** (or 2MWT if endurance is very limited)
- **Dynamic Gait Index (DGI)**
- **30 Second Sit-to-Stand (30STS)**
- **Functional Gait Assessment (FGA)** or **Berg Balance Scale** where available

## Participation / confidence

- **Patient Specific Functional Scale (PSFS)** — participant nominates their own priority activities (e.g. "walk to local shops," "use stairs") and rates each 0–10 at baseline and end of trial. Very persuasive to assessors because it's the participant's own goals, not a generic scale.
- **Activities-specific Balance Confidence Scale (ABC)**
- **Falls Efficacy Scale (FES)**
- **Fear of Falling Avoidance Questionnaire**

## Falls / safety record

- Falls and near-falls in the last 3–6 months, with description of circumstances.

## Gait observation (qualitative but report explicitly)

Toe drag, stumbling episodes, hip hiking, circumduction, knee hyperextension in stance, foot inversion/eversion during swing, heel-strike consistency, and where in the gait cycle each occurs.
