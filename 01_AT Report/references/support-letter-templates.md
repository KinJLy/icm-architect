# Support letter templates — community trial / device funding request

Three author variants below. Pick the one matching who's signing; the clinical content (Section 2, functional impact) is the same shape regardless of author.

## Checklist (applies to every variant)

- [ ] Your name, role, qualifications, registration number, organisation, contact details, date
- [ ] Participant's full name and NDIS number
- [ ] Your relationship to the participant and how long you've supported them
- [ ] Current supports provided (type, frequency) and any informal supports (family/community)
- [ ] Functional impact across relevant NDIS domains — mobility, self-care, self-management, communication, social interaction, learning — with **concrete examples**, not just diagnostic language
- [ ] Why current supports/equipment are insufficient
- [ ] Why a trial (not straight purchase) is the appropriate next step, and what it will evaluate
- [ ] How the trial will be monitored (measures to be collected, safety supervision)
- [ ] Risks if not approved
- [ ] Explicit statement: "reasonable and necessary"
- [ ] Signature, credentials, offer to provide further information

## Variant: generic / allied health

```
[Your name] / [role] / [organisation] / [contact]
[Date]

Re: Support for a community trial of [device] for [participant], NDIS number [if known]

I am writing in my capacity as [role] and have worked with [participant] for [duration].

1. Background & Current Support
[Diagnosis/condition and resulting impairment]. Over [period], I provide [therapy/support type] for [frequency]. [Participant] also receives support from [family/carers/services].

2. Functional Impact & Limitations
Mobility: [specific example, e.g. "trips or requires rest breaks walking >50m on uneven footpaths"]
Self-care: [specific example]
Self-management: [specific example]
Social interaction: [specific example, if relevant]

3. Justification for Trial
A community trial of [device] is warranted because it has the potential to improve functional mobility, reduce fatigue, and increase community participation. A real-world trial will determine whether it represents value for money for inclusion in the NDIS plan. Without it, [participant] risks continued limitation, safety risk, and reduced independence.

4. Monitoring & Safety
The trial will be supervised, with [outcome measures] collected, and monitoring for [discomfort/skin/safety issues].

5. Recommendation
In my professional opinion, this trial is reasonable and necessary for [participant].

[Signature / credentials / contact]
```

## Variant: orthotist assessor

Same shape as above, but Section 2 leads with orthotic history specifically:

```
2. Current Orthotic Management
[Participant] has trialled [specific AFO/device], which provided [limited benefit] but presented [specific limitations — restricted movement, energy cost, fit, tolerance]. Despite this, [participant] continues to experience [tripping/reduced endurance/dependence].

3. Justification for Trial
[Device] applies [mechanism] to [specific functional target]. A community trial is necessary because clinical/in-clinic testing alone cannot reflect day-to-day demands — this ensures NDIS investment in high-cost technology is supported by functional evidence, not projection.
```

Sign off as: `[Name], Orthotist – Assessor (AOPA Registration # [xxxxxx])`.

## Variant: physiotherapist

Same shape, Section 2 leads with therapy history and objective measures already collected (gait training progress, strength/balance trends) rather than device-fitting history.
