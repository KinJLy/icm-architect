# P — Clinical justification for custom-made AFOs

**Participant:** P [Surname]
**Assessor:** [Orthotist name]

## AFO history

- P has worn AFOs since approximately age 5.
- Current AFOs fitted **April 2024**, following cracking of the previous pair.
- Previous AFOs fitted **November 2021**.

## Longevity

Longevity is difficult to specify exactly — it varies significantly between individuals. Some patients have used devices for over five years with only minor repairs; others require replacement within a year due to physiological change (e.g. weight fluctuation, surgery). Additional factors affecting lifespan: UV exposure (degrades plastic over time), frequency/duration of wear, activity level and gait demands.

## Repair vs replacement

- **Repairable:** straps, padding, and other minor wear-and-tear components.
- **Not repairable:** structural issues such as plastic cracking (as occurred with P's previous pair) or changes in limb shape — these require fabrication of a new custom AFO to maintain alignment and safety.

## Why custom, not a lower-cost off-the-shelf option

**Not appropriate for P.** Due to foot deformities and bony prominences, off-the-shelf AFOs will not fit properly and are not viable — see `../../../references/evidence-summary-cp-afo.md` for the supporting literature on why CP gait patterns and anatomy require individualised (not generic) device selection.

## Clinical justification

Custom-made AFOs support P's posture and gait alignment, facilitate safe and efficient mobility, improve balance, and reduce fatigue during ambulation. Without them, P tires early and experiences loss of balance, increasing fall risk and dependence on support workers. Poorly fitted or generic AFOs would compromise function, comfort, and safety, and would not support P's functional NDIS goals.

Without AFOs, P would be at increased risk of:
- Early fatigue and balance deterioration
- Increased strain on support workers
- Premature progression to greater reliance on a wheelchair

## Summary

Given P's presentation, history of long-term AFO use, and the clinical evidence supporting orthotic intervention, custom-made AFOs remain the most appropriate and necessary orthotic solution. Off-the-shelf options are not suitable due to anatomical and biomechanical requirements; replacement is required per current clinical findings and material wear.
