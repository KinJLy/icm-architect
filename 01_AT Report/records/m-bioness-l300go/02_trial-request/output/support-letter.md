# NDIS Support Letter — Application for Community Trial of Bioness L300 Go (FES System)

**Participant:** M [Surname]
**Date of Birth:** [DD/MM/YYYY]
**Diagnosis:** Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
**Referring Therapist:** [Physiotherapist name, if applicable]
**Orthotist:** [Name], Certified Orthotist/Prosthetist

## Purpose

This letter is written in support of funding a community trial of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) system for M, a 35-year-old woman with Multiple Sclerosis. M presents with significant right-sided foot drop and gait deviations, which impact her safety, mobility, and independence in daily and community activities.

## Clinical presentation

See `../01_intake-assessment/output/assessment-findings.md` for full detail. Summary: right ankle dorsiflexion weakness (with a noted assessor discrepancy — see that file), eversion 0/5, inversion 1/5, moderate gastroc/soleus spasticity, right knee hyperextension in stance, foot drop and inversion in mid-swing, independent indoors with a 4-wheeled walker.

## Previous orthotic intervention

M trialled an off-the-shelf OAPL Supra-Lite AFO, which proved unsuccessful and ill-fitting due to the combination of spasticity, mild contracture, and limb shape; she also reported discomfort and difficulty tolerating its bulkiness. A custom-made AFO was being considered through Korthotics; orthotic funding was not, at the time, included in M's NDIS plan.

## In-clinic FES trial

M attended Korthotics on 24 September 2025 with her physiotherapist and mother for an in-clinic trial of the Bioness L300 Go. The device applies targeted stimulation to the common peroneal nerve, producing ankle dorsiflexion during swing phase and assisting a more natural gait pattern.

During the short in-clinic trial, M demonstrated:
- Noticeable improvement in foot clearance during swing
- Improved stability in stance phase
- Increased walking speed and distance (approximately five times her baseline walking distance without requiring a rest break)
- Reduced fatigue and improved walking confidence

## Clinical rationale

The Bioness L300 Go offers both orthotic (immediate) and therapeutic (long-term) benefits — see `../../../references/evidence-summary-ms-fes.md`. Given M has not tolerated conventional orthoses successfully, FES presents a functionally suitable and potentially superior alternative to traditional AFO management.

## Recommendation

It is recommended that the NDIS fund a community-based trial of the Bioness L300 Go FES system for M, to evaluate real-world functional impact on walking and fatigue management, determine long-term suitability, and ensure any future device funding is clinically justified and cost-effective before proceeding to a full prescription.
