# M — clinical assessment findings

35-year-old woman, diagnosis of Multiple Sclerosis (MS), right-sided presentation.

## Muscle strength (Manual Muscle Testing)

| Muscle group / joint | Strength | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Right ankle dorsiflexion | 0/5 (physiotherapy) — see discrepancy note below | Primary contributor to reduced foot clearance |
| Right ankle dorsiflexion | ~3/5 (neurological assessment, later review) | Recorded at a different timepoint/context — see below |
| Right ankle plantarflexion | 3/5 | Weakness / spasticity noted |
| Right ankle eversion | 0/5 | No active lateral control |
| Right ankle inversion | 1/5 | Minimal flicker of activation |
| Right knee flexion/extension | 4/5 | |
| Right hip abduction/extension | 5/5 | |

**Discrepancy — DF strength 0/5 vs ~3/5:** two different assessors recorded different dorsiflexion scores at different times. Do not treat either number in isolation or silently pick one — follow the reasoning and phrasing pattern in `../../../references/justification-banks/assessment-discrepancy-reasoning.md`. The functional finding (consistent foot drop) held regardless of which numeric score was recorded, and the report should lead with that.

## Range of motion

- Right ankle dorsiflexion with knee extended: approximately −3° (mild equinus positioning).
- No contractures significant enough to preclude FES.

## Tone / spasticity

- Moderate spasticity: gastrocnemius and soleus (gastrocnemius > soleus).
- Mild spasticity: ankle invertors.
- Contributes to foot inversion, toe drag, reduced walking efficiency, increased energy expenditure during gait.

## Additional findings

- Right-sided toe curling evident with prolonged standing/walking.
- Independent mobility indoors with a 4-wheeled walker.

## Gait assessment

- **Initial contact:** inconsistent heel strike.
- **Swing phase:** significant foot drop, reduced foot clearance, ankle inversion, increased risk of catching the foot while walking.
- **Stance phase:** right knee hyperextension, reduced shock absorption, increased stress through the knee joint.
- **Overall pattern:** slower, cautious gait; increased concentration while walking; reduced efficiency; increased fatigue; reduced confidence.

## Functional impact

Reported difficulty with: walking longer distances, standing for extended periods, community access, household tasks, family activities, walking on uneven surfaces.

Note the framing that matters for the report: these limitations do not arise solely because M is unable to walk — they arise because walking requires considerable effort and fatigue management, resulting in progressive restriction of meaningful activities. (See `../../../references/report-block-structure.md` on reframing low fall incidence the same way.)

## Previous AT and why it was insufficient

Trialled an off-the-shelf OAPL Supra-Lite AFO — unsuccessful and ill-fitting, likely due to spasticity combined with mild contracture; participant also reported disliking its bulkiness. A custom AFO was being sourced through Korthotics at the time of this assessment, but orthotic funding was not yet included in M's NDIS plan.

## Fatigue

Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS) completed 21 January 2026: **63/63** (maximum on all 9 items, average 7.0/7) — indicates maximum measurable fatigue burden. See `../../../references/outcome-measures.md` for how to frame this.
