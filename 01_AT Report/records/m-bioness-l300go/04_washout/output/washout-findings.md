# Washout findings — M, Bioness L300 Go (3-week withdrawal period)

## Post-Bioness Withdrawal Analysis (3-week washout)

Following completion of the 8-week community trial, the participant
returned the Bioness L300 Go and completed a structured 3-week
withdrawal ("washout") period to evaluate changes in function without
access to the device.

The participant demonstrated a progressive deterioration in mobility,
fatigue, confidence, safety and community participation throughout the
washout period. Six themes emerged from her logbook entries during this
period:

### 1. Increased physical fatigue

The participant consistently reported severe leg soreness, calf
tightness, muscle stiffness, ankle weakness, increased effort required
for walking, and prolonged recovery periods after activities.

Examples recorded:

> "Woke up with extremely sore body and legs especially my right leg."

> "Legs very sore and stiff."

### 2. Increased instability and trips

Near-falls and trips re-emerged during the washout period. Documented
examples include tripping while exiting the bathroom, tripping through a
doorway, scuffed toes from inadequate foot clearance, and repeated
tripping over her own feet.

The participant described this as:

> "Almost completely falling."

> "Keep tripping over my own feet."

### 3. Significant reduction in walking quality

Throughout the washout period, the participant repeatedly rated her
mobility as difficult, very difficult, worse, or much worse than when
using the Bioness.

She reported:

> "Walking was very slow."

> "Not very confident."

> "Hard to walk."

> "Takes me forever to move."

### 4. Increased cognitive load

The participant described having to consciously think about every
movement again. Without the Bioness, mobility became mentally demanding
and exhausting. She reported needing greater concentration for foot
placement, increased conscious awareness of foot position to avoid
falls, and loss of the automatic gait patterning she had while using the
device.

### 5. Reduced community participation

Community participation became substantially harder during the washout
period. Examples include difficulty visiting nursing homes, slower
movement in community environments, increased need for rest periods, and
reduced confidence about leaving home.

### 6. Psychological deterioration

This theme is flagged in the source material as an especially powerful
piece of evidence for NDIS purposes. The participant reported:

> "I NEED my Bioness."

> "Depressed, unhappy."

> "I can't cope with this pain anymore."

The participant reported needing psychology support due to deteriorating
mental wellbeing associated with the loss of independent mobility.

### Clinical interpretation (as stated in the source)

The withdrawal period provides evidence that the participant's
improvements observed during the 8-week Bioness L300 Go trial were
directly attributable to the device rather than natural symptom
fluctuation, placebo effect, concurrent physiotherapy intervention, or
general disease variability. Upon removal of the device, a clear and
progressive decline was observed across multiple domains — walking
safety, endurance, fatigue, confidence, balance, community participation
and psychological wellbeing. This pattern is presented in the source as
support for the conclusion that ongoing access to the Bioness L300 Go is
necessary to maintain the participant's functional capacity and prevent
significant deterioration in daily life.

*(Note: this is the source material's own clinical framing/argument, not
an independently verified clinical conclusion — it should be reviewed
against the underlying washout logbook data before being relied upon
as-is.)*
