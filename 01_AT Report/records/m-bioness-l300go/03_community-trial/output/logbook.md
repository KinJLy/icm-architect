# M — Bioness L300 Go Community Trial Logbook (raw entries)

This is primary case evidence: M's own reported diary entries during the
8-week community trial of the Bioness L300 Go, as captured (in translated/
cleaned form) from the source chat export. Wording has been translated to
English and lightly cleaned for readability, but activities, distances,
step counts, timings, and incidents are preserved as reported — nothing
has been paraphrased away or summarised out.

## Device training-graph analysis (Week 3 vs Week 4)

The Bioness device's own usage analytics (training graph) recorded time
spent in "Training Mode" for the Upper Leg (Thigh) and Lower Leg
components, by session, across Week 3 and Week 4:

**Week 3 — initial adaptation period**

| Component | Session 1 | Session 2 | Session 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upper Leg (Thigh) | 2.14 min | 5.86 min | 2.71 min |
| Lower Leg | 4.14 min | 7.71 min | 2.71 min |

**Week 4**

| Component | Session 1 | Session 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Upper Leg (Thigh) | 0.97 min | 2.03 min |
| Lower Leg | 2.13 min | 2.43 min |

**Interpretation (as recorded in the source):** Training Mode usage was
highest in Week 3 and fell in Week 4. The source material's own analysis
frames this as a positive sign, not a negative one — the drop reflects a
shift away from device *training* (stimulation adaptation, intensity
adjustment, programme familiarisation) and toward the *functional
walking mode* used during real community ambulation, i.e. a transition
from device training to functional community use. Suggested report
sentence carried over from the source: "Device analytics demonstrated
that training mode utilisation was highest during the initial adaptation
phase and progressively reduced as the participant became familiar with
the system. This pattern is consistent with successful transition from
device training to functional community use."

Note: this is the *training-graph* analytics only. The source separately
flags that the fuller "Bioness Clinician Programmer" usage analytics
(weekly usage, steps/cadence, stride proportions, training-vs-walking
mode split) was identified as missing from the report's Appendix — see
the submission-compliance checklist for that finding.
