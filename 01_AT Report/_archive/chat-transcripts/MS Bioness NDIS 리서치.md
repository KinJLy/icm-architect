1. 핵심 근거 요약 (MS + FES/Bioness)
✔ 1) FES는 MS foot drop의 표준 대체 치료 옵션

연구들은 MS 환자의 발처짐(foot drop)에 대해 FES(functional electrical stimulation) 가 기존 AFO(ankle-foot orthosis)와 함께 대표적인 보조 치료임을 보고함.

FES는 비골신경(peroneal nerve)을 자극하여
→ 발목 배측굴곡(dorsiflexion) 유도
→ 스윙기 toe clearance 개선

📌 근거:

MS 환자에서 FES는 “orthotic alternative to AFO”로 사용됨
보행 중 보다 자연스러운 gait pattern 제공
✔ 2) 보행 속도 및 기능 향상 (객관적 결과)

여러 임상시험 및 메타분석에서 FES 사용 시 다음 개선이 반복적으로 확인됨:

FES는 단순 보행 보조를 넘어 기능적 참여에도 영향:

2. NDIS AT Report에 바로 넣을 수 있는 문장 (핵심)

아래는 그대로 복사 가능한 “임상 근거 문장”입니다:

✔ Evidence-Based Clinical Rationale (MS + Bioness FES)

Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES), including devices such as the Bioness L300, is an evidence-based intervention for the management of foot drop in individuals with Multiple Sclerosis (MS). FES facilitates dorsiflexion during swing phase by stimulating the peroneal nerve, improving toe clearance and promoting a more physiological gait pattern.

Clinical studies and systematic reviews demonstrate that FES use in MS is associated with improvements in gait speed, walking endurance, and functional mobility, including clinically meaningful changes in timed walking tests and 6-minute walk performance. In addition, improvements in patient-reported outcomes such as walking ability and health-related quality of life have been consistently reported.

Systematic reviews further indicate that FES provides positive orthotic effects on gait in MS, with many studies showing significant improvements in walking speed and mobility-related outcomes compared with no device or conventional orthotic management.

Longer-term use of FES has also been associated with improvements in neuromuscular control and reduced fatigue-related gait deterioration, supporting its role not only as an orthotic aid but also as a gait re-education tool.

NDIS Evidence Summary & Clinical Rationale
Bioness L300 Go (Functional Electrical Stimulation System) – MS Population
1. Device Overview (Upgrade Context: L300 → L300 Go)

The Bioness L300 Go is an advanced Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) system designed to improve gait in individuals with upper motor neuron conditions, including Multiple Sclerosis (MS).

Compared to the earlier L300 model, the L300 Go provides:

Integrated 3D motion detection (no external foot switch required)
Improved real-time gait adaptation
Thigh cuff option for knee control (flexion/extension support)
Simplified setup and mobility use across environments
App-based monitoring and adjustment for clinical tracking

The system delivers stimulation to the peroneal nerve, enabling ankle dorsiflexion during swing phase, improving toe clearance and gait mechanics.

2. Clinical Indications (MS Relevant)

The L300 Go is indicated for individuals with:

Foot drop
Muscle weakness due to upper motor neuron lesions
Gait instability secondary to neurological disease

It is specifically designed for populations including Multiple Sclerosis, stroke, and other neurological conditions affecting gait.

In MS, impairments commonly include:

Reduced dorsiflexion
Fatigue-related gait deterioration
Reduced walking endurance
Compensatory gait patterns increasing falls risk
3. Evidence Summary – MS and FES (L300 / L300 Go)
3.1 Gait improvement

Clinical and observational studies show that FES use in MS is associated with:

Increased walking speed (10MWT improvement)
Improved gait symmetry
Reduced toe drag and improved clearance
Improved functional mobility scores (e.g., TUG, MSWS-12)
3.2 Functional mobility & endurance

FES has been shown to:

Improve walking endurance (6MWT gains)
Reduce energy cost of walking
Improve tolerance for community ambulation
Support longer-distance walking with reduced fatigue
3.3 Fatigue-related gait deterioration (key MS factor)

A key advantage in MS is that FES:

Maintains dorsiflexion during fatigue states
Reduces deterioration of gait quality over time
Supports more consistent walking performance across the day
3.4 Neuroplasticity and motor re-education

Evidence suggests repeated FES use may:

Support motor relearning through repetitive activation
Facilitate cortical re-engagement of gait patterns
Contribute to long-term functional maintenance in some users
4. Evidence of Functional Outcomes (L300 Go specific findings)

Clinical outcome data and case-based evidence report:

Improved gait mechanics after short training periods (6–8 sessions)
Improved step length and reduced toe drag
Improved Timed Up and Go (TUG)
Improved 10 Metre Walk Test (10MWT)
Improved gait stability under fatigue conditions

Example findings from clinical case series:

Faster walking speed and improved balance control after 6 weeks of use
Reduced compensatory hip hiking and circumduction patterns
Improved heel strike and swing phase symmetry
5. Comparison: AFO vs FES (NDIS-relevant justification)
Feature	AFO	FES (Bioness L300 Go)
Gait pattern	Mechanical restriction	Physiological gait activation
Muscle activation	None	Active dorsiflexor recruitment
Fatigue adaptation	Limited	Dynamic adjustment during fatigue
Terrain adaptability	Limited	Higher adaptability
Neuro-retraining effect	No	Yes (motor re-education potential)
Walking efficiency	Variable	Often improved
6. Clinical Rationale for Upgrade (L300 → L300 Go)

Upgrading from earlier L300 models to L300 Go is clinically justified due to:

6.1 Improved gait responsiveness
Real-time motion sensing removes need for external foot switch
More accurate stimulation timing during swing phase
6.2 Increased functional independence
Easier donning/doffing improves daily use compliance
Reduced setup complexity supports community use
6.3 Expanded functional control
Optional thigh cuff provides knee control for MS patients with proximal weakness or fatigue-related instability
6.4 Better long-term usability
App-based adjustments allow ongoing optimisation as MS symptoms fluctuate
7. Functional Impact in MS (Clinical relevance for NDIS)

Without FES support, MS-related foot drop typically results in:

Increased falls risk
Reduced community participation
Increased fatigue and reduced walking distance
Compensatory gait leading to secondary musculoskeletal strain

With L300 Go use, expected functional improvements include:

Increased safety during ambulation
Improved community mobility
Reduced fatigue burden
Improved independence in daily activities
Improved confidence and participation
8. NDIS Reasonable & Necessary Justification

The Bioness L300 Go is considered reasonable and necessary because it:

Directly addresses functional impairment (foot drop)
Improves mobility and safety
Reduces falls risk
Supports participation in community and daily life
Provides measurable functional improvements in gait outcomes
Is evidence-based for MS populations

It is also cost-effective in reducing long-term support needs associated with falls, fatigue-related inactivity, and mobility decline.

9. Conclusion

The Bioness L300 Go is a clinically supported Functional Electrical Stimulation device for individuals with Multiple Sclerosis presenting with foot drop.

Evidence supports its effectiveness in improving gait speed, walking endurance, and functional mobility while reducing fatigue-related gait deterioration.

Upgrading from earlier L300 systems to L300 Go is clinically justified due to improved gait responsiveness, usability, and functional adaptability.

The device is therefore recommended as appropriate, evidence-based assistive technology under NDIS funding criteria.

Foot drop
Knee instability (optional thigh cuff control)
Fatigue-related gait deterioration

Multi-channel stimulation (lower limb + thigh control option)
Improved knee flexion/extension support during gait

Multiple Sclerosis commonly results in:

Weakness of ankle dorsiflexors (foot drop)
Reduced selective motor control
Spasticity and abnormal tone
Fatigue-related deterioration in gait quality
Reduced walking endurance and increased falls risk

FES systems such as the L300 Go Plus directly address these impairments by:

Facilitating timed dorsiflexion during swing phase
Improving knee control in cases of proximal weakness or fatigue

Increased gait speed (10MWT improvements)

Improve 6-minute walk distance (6MWT)

Maintains dorsiflexion even under fatigue

Reinforcement of motor pathways through repetitive activation
