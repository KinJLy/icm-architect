1.5 – 3 hours total

Evidence-based rationale

Translating clinical findings → NDIS language
Aligning with NDIS “reasonable and necessary” criteria
Linking impairments → functional limitations → participation impact

💰 1. NDIS Billing: What time can you actually charge?

Under the NDIS framework, AT report writing usually falls under:

✔️ Capacity Building – Improved Daily Living

(Line item typically used by orthotists)

🧾 What is generally billable

You can legitimately bill for:

Clinical assessment (face-to-face or telehealth)
AT assessment & trials
Report writing (clinical justification)
Liaising with other clinicians (OT, physio, GP)
Reviewing outcomes / follow-up documentation

👉 In practice:

Report writing IS billable, as long as it is clinically necessary and documented
⚠️ What often becomes non-billable (or risky to claim)
Excessive admin (copy-paste / formatting)
Rewriting due to poor initial documentation
Long back-and-forth emails not tied to clinical reasoning
Rejected reports requiring full rewrite (sometimes disputed)
💵 Typical billing pattern (real-world)

For a standard orthotic AT report:

Assessment: 1–2 hrs
Report writing: 1.5–3 hrs
Liaising: 0.5–1 hr

➡️ Total billed: ~3–5 hours

For complex cases (ALS, MS, CP, KAFO):

Total can go up to 5–8+ hours
💡 Key compliance tip

NDIS planners look for:

Clear link: impairment → functional limitation → participation restriction
Why this AT (and not cheaper alternatives)
Risks if not funded

If those are weak → report gets rejected → more unpaid time

⚡ 2. How to cut report time by 30–50% (practical workflow)

This is where most orthotists lose time. Templates alone don’t fix it—you need a structured system.

🔧 Step-by-step efficient workflow
1. Capture “NDIS-ready” notes during assessment (biggest time saver)

Instead of writing:

“Foot drop, reduced DF”

Write directly in NDIS language:

“Left foot drop resulting in reduced toe clearance, increasing falls risk and limiting safe community ambulation”

👉 This alone can cut 30–40% of writing time

2. Use a modular template (not one big document)

Break your report into reusable blocks:

Condition summary (e.g. MS / CP / ALS variants)
Functional impact paragraphs
Orthotic justification (AFO / KAFO / carbon)
Risk of non-provision
Alternatives trialled

👉 Then you just select + tweak, not rewrite

3. Pre-write common justification banks

Example:

Carbon AFO justification (reusable core):

Energy return → improves gait efficiency
Reduces fatigue
Enables longer community participation

Then customise 20–30% only

4. Use a “3-layer writing method”

Instead of writing perfectly in one go:

Layer 1 (10–15 min): dump key points
Layer 2 (30–60 min): structure into sections
Layer 3 (20–30 min): polish for NDIS language

👉 Faster than trying to write perfectly from the start

5. Standardise objective phrases

Have ready-to-use lines like:

“Without this device, the participant is at increased risk of falls and injury”
“This limitation significantly impacts independent mobility and community access”
“A lower-cost alternative is not suitable due to…”

👉 Saves surprising amounts of time

6. Quote + spec automation
Save common device specs (AFO, KAFO, BSAFO, carbon)
Pre-fill supplier details
7. Final 10-minute compliance check (prevents rework)

Before submitting, quickly confirm:

✔️ Is it clearly “reasonable & necessary”?
✔️ Have you ruled out cheaper options?
✔️ Is functional impact strong enough?
✔️ Is risk clearly stated?

👉 This step prevents hours of unpaid rewrites
