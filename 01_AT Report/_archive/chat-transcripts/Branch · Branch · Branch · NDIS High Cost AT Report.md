EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Purpose of Report

This report has been prepared to support an application for NDIS funding of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) System, including both Lower Leg and Thigh Components, for M, a 35-year-old woman with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) and significant right lower limb impairment resulting in foot drop, gait instability, fatigue and participation restrictions.

The report presents findings from a comprehensive clinical assessment, an in-clinic trial, an eight-week community trial, and a subsequent three-week washout period. Objective outcome measures, participant feedback, carer observations and specialist medical evidence have been used to determine whether the requested assistive technology represents a reasonable and necessary support under Section 34 of the NDIS Act 2013.

Participant Overview

M has a diagnosis of Multiple Sclerosis and presents with significant right-sided foot drop, impaired foot clearance during swing phase, gait instability, knee hyperextension, fatigue and reduced walking endurance.

Clinical assessment identified absent right ankle dorsiflexion (0/5), absent eversion (0/5), reduced plantarflexion strength (3/5), mild ankle inversion strength (1/5), ankle dorsiflexion restriction and neurological gait abnormalities consistent with MS-related foot drop.

Although M remains ambulant with a four-wheeled walker, her mobility is significantly impacted by fatigue, instability and reduced confidence when walking in community environments.

M does not require the Bioness L300 Go because she is unable to walk. Rather, she requires the device because she currently maintains mobility only through significant activity restriction, compensatory strategies, reduced participation and avoidance of meaningful activities. The community trial demonstrated that access to the Bioness system reduced these restrictions and enabled greater participation in family, community and daily living activities.

Community Trial Outcomes

An eight-week community trial of the Bioness L300 Go was completed between March and May 2026, followed by a three-week washout period.

Objective outcomes demonstrated clinically meaningful improvements during active device use, including:

58% improvement in Six Minute Walk Test distance (57m to 90m)
22% improvement in Timed Up and Go performance
Improved Dynamic Gait Index scores
Improved standing tolerance
Improved gait quality
Reduced knee hyperextension
Improved walking confidence

Participant-reported outcomes identified improvements in:

Community access
Family participation
Confidence walking outdoors
Cooking and baking activities
Attendance at appointments
General quality of life

The participant's treating neurologist also documented improvements in mobility, endurance, confidence and quality of life during the trial period.

Washout Findings

Following removal of the device, M experienced deterioration across multiple functional domains.

Observed changes included:

Reduced walking confidence
Increased fatigue
Reduced community participation
Return of foot drag
Return of knee hyperextension symptoms
Increased activity avoidance
Four trip and near-fall events within three weeks

The washout findings confirmed that the improvements observed during the community trial were directly attributable to the Bioness system.

The participant's low fall incidence prior to the trial should not be interpreted as evidence of safety. Rather, it reflects the extent to which M had restricted her activities and modified her behaviour to avoid situations that increased her risk of tripping, fatigue and loss of balance.

Clinical Conclusion

The collective evidence demonstrates that the Bioness L300 Go provides clinically significant and meaningful benefits for M across mobility, participation, confidence and quality of life domains.

The recommendation is based not only on objective outcome measures but also on demonstrated real-world functional improvements observed during community use and subsequent deterioration following device removal.

The device represents the most appropriate assistive technology option currently available to address M's disability-related mobility limitations.

Recommendation Summary

Based on assessment findings, community trial outcomes, washout findings, participant feedback, carer observations and neurologist endorsement, NDIS funding is strongly recommended for:

Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System

including:

Lower Leg Component
Thigh Component
Initial Assessment and Fitting
Clinical Programming
Clinical Reviews
Device Optimisation
Ongoing Clinical Support

The recommended support satisfies the requirements of Section 34 of the NDIS Act 2013 and is considered reasonable, necessary and value for money.

6.6 Recommendation Statement

It is my professional opinion that funding of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System is clinically justified, evidence-based, and necessary to address the disability-related mobility limitations experienced by M.

M does not require the Bioness L300 Go because she is unable to walk. Rather, she requires the device because she currently maintains mobility through significant compensatory effort, activity restriction, fatigue management strategies and avoidance of meaningful activities. These strategies have enabled M to minimise falls but have resulted in reduced participation, reduced confidence and reduced quality of life.

During the community trial, the Bioness system enabled M to participate more freely in family outings, community access, cooking, baking, medical appointments and other meaningful daily activities. Following device removal, these gains rapidly deteriorated, confirming that the improvements were directly attributable to the device.

The device demonstrated measurable improvements in walking endurance, gait efficiency, standing tolerance, confidence and community participation. These improvements were consistently supported by objective outcome measures, participant feedback, carer observations and neurologist endorsement.

The subsequent washout period confirmed that these benefits were directly attributable to the device and were not maintained following its removal.

Without access to the device, M is likely to continue relying on significant activity restriction as her primary risk management strategy, resulting in reduced participation, reduced independence and increased reliance on informal supports.

The recommendation is therefore based on demonstrated functional benefit rather than theoretical benefit.

The Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System satisfies all requirements of Section 34 of the NDIS Act 2013 and represents the most appropriate assistive technology currently available to address M's identified functional needs.

Accordingly, I strongly recommend NDIS funding approval for the purchase of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System, including both the Lower Leg and Thigh Components, together with all associated clinical services required for successful long-term implementation.
