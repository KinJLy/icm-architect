EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Participant

M is a 35-year-old woman with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) who experiences significant mobility limitations resulting from right-sided foot drop, gait instability, neurological fatigue, reduced walking endurance and impaired balance. These impairments substantially affect her ability to safely and confidently participate in everyday activities, community access and family life.

M mobilises using a four-wheeled walker and has developed numerous compensatory strategies to manage her mobility limitations. Despite these strategies, she continues to experience considerable restrictions in participation and activity engagement due to the physical and cognitive demands associated with walking.

Importantly, M does not require the Bioness L300 Go because she is unable to walk. Rather, she requires the device because she currently maintains mobility only through significant activity restriction, compensatory strategies, reduced participation and avoidance of meaningful activities. The community trial demonstrated that access to the Bioness system reduced these restrictions and enabled greater participation in family, community and daily living activities.

Assessment Findings

Comprehensive orthotic assessment identified neurological impairments consistent with Multiple Sclerosis affecting the right lower limb, including foot drop, reduced motor control, gait instability and knee hyperextension during stance.

Clinical findings demonstrated:

Severe impairment of right ankle dorsiflexion function
Right foot drop during swing phase
Reduced foot clearance resulting in increased trip risk
Right knee hyperextension during stance
Neurological fatigue affecting walking capacity
Reduced balance confidence
Significant participation restrictions in community activities

These impairments directly impact M's mobility, safety, endurance and ability to participate in everyday life.

Alternative interventions including physiotherapy, mobility aids and orthotic management were considered. While these interventions provide partial benefit, they do not adequately address the combined effects of foot drop, gait inefficiency, fatigue and activity restriction demonstrated during assessment.

Trial Summary

Following a successful in-clinic trial, M completed an eight-week community trial of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) System incorporating both the Lower Leg and Thigh Components.

The community trial demonstrated clinically meaningful improvements in mobility, endurance, standing tolerance, confidence and participation.

Objective outcome measures demonstrated:

Timed Up and Go improved from 42.57 seconds to 33.11 seconds (22% improvement)
10 Metre Walk Test improved from 35.19 seconds to 29.31 seconds (17% improvement)
6 Minute Walk Test improved from 57 metres to 90 metres (58% improvement)
Dynamic Gait Index improved from 11/24 to 14/24
30 Second Sit-to-Stand improved from 9 to 10 repetitions

Participant-specific goals also demonstrated meaningful improvement throughout the active trial period.

Importantly, the greatest benefits observed were not limited to objective gait measures. M reported significant improvements in confidence, participation and quality of life, including increased ability to attend medical appointments, participate in family outings, access the community, cook, bake and engage in meaningful daily activities.

M described feeling more stable, more confident and less fearful of walking, while her mother reported significant improvements in confidence, participation and emotional wellbeing.

Washout Findings

Following completion of the community trial, M returned the device and completed a structured three-week washout period.

During this period, many of the gains achieved during the active trial deteriorated.

Washout findings included:

Return of foot drag and reduced foot clearance
Return of knee hyperextension
Reduced walking confidence
Increased fatigue
Increased activity avoidance
Reduced participation in family and community activities
Four trip or near-fall incidents over a three-week period

M reported increased anxiety, reduced confidence and avoidance of activities she had been able to undertake during the active trial period.

The washout findings demonstrated that the improvements observed during the community trial were directly attributable to the Bioness system and were not maintained following device removal.

Importantly, M's low fall incidence prior to the trial should not be interpreted as evidence of safety. Rather, it reflects the extent to which she restricted her activities and modified her behaviour to minimise risk. The washout period demonstrated that when mobility demands increased, functional limitations and safety concerns quickly re-emerged.

Clinical Conclusion

The combined findings from clinical assessment, in-clinic trial, eight-week community trial and three-week washout period provide strong evidence that the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System delivers substantial and meaningful functional benefit for M.

The device demonstrated measurable improvements in:

Functional mobility
Walking endurance
Standing tolerance
Confidence
Community participation
Family participation
Quality of life

The evidence indicates that the Bioness system enables M to participate more fully in everyday activities while reducing the activity restrictions and compensatory strategies currently required to maintain mobility.

Recommendation

Based on the totality of evidence presented within this report, it is my professional opinion that the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System is clinically justified, effective and necessary to address M's disability-related mobility limitations arising from Multiple Sclerosis.

The requested assistive technology satisfies the requirements of Section 34 of the National Disability Insurance Scheme Act 2013 and represents the most appropriate assistive technology intervention currently available to address M's identified functional needs.

Accordingly, NDIS funding is strongly recommended for:

Bioness L300 Go Lower Leg Component
Bioness L300 Go Thigh Component
Initial fitting and programming
Clinical review and optimisation appointments
Ongoing orthotic support associated with device implementation

The recommendation is supported by objective outcome measures, real-world community trial evidence, washout findings, participant feedback, carer observations and specialist medical endorsement.

1. PURPOSE OF REPORT

This report has been prepared by the prescribing Orthotist to assess the suitability of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) System for M, a 35-year-old woman with Multiple Sclerosis (MS), and to determine whether funding of the recommended device represents a reasonable and necessary support under Section 34 of the National Disability Insurance Scheme Act 2013.

M experiences significant disability-related mobility limitations associated with right-sided foot drop, gait instability, neurological fatigue and reduced walking endurance. These impairments affect her ability to safely and effectively participate in daily activities, community access and family life.

The purpose of this report is to:

Document M's disability-related functional impairments and mobility limitations.
Present the findings of comprehensive orthotic assessment.
Evaluate alternative interventions that have been trialled or considered.
Present the outcomes of the in-clinic trial, eight-week community trial and three-week washout period.
Review objective outcome measures, participant feedback and specialist medical evidence.
Assess the requested assistive technology against NDIS Reasonable and Necessary criteria.
Provide a clinical recommendation regarding funding of the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System.

The recommendation contained within this report is based on a combination of clinical assessment findings, real-world trial outcomes, participant-specific goals, published evidence and observed changes following device removal during the washout period.

Importantly, the recommendation is not based solely on gait impairment or fall risk. Rather, it reflects the substantial impact that M's mobility limitations have on participation, confidence, activity engagement and quality of life.

The report demonstrates that the Bioness L300 Go Functional Electrical Stimulation System provides meaningful functional benefit and addresses disability-related barriers that cannot be adequately managed through existing supports alone.
